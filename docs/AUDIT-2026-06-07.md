## Worth building this week

Medium-effort, high-leverage UX wins. Each is one to three hours of build time.

### 1. /random should preserve the joy
Currently /random shows "Loading..." and silently redirects. A reader hits it and lands on a random entry with no sense of selection. **Fix:** flash the picked subject for 1.5s with "Today's pick: Hippopotamus" then redirect. Or, better, render the random entry IN PLACE without redirect, with a "Pick another" button. Increases pages-per-session.

### 2. Tag cloud is bloated (92/116 singletons)
116 unique tags across 55 entries; 92 are used exactly once. Tag pages with one entry are dead ends. **Fix:** consolidate to ~20 canonical tags (industrial, vegetable, vehicle, instrument, architecture, etc.). Backfill mapping in `data/entries.json`. Tag pages become real navigation.

### 3. Hissar sheep + Goose, Canada - pull both, backfill
Editorial-sanity flags two animal violations now (Hissar sheep 4/12, Goose, Canada 6/3). Both are outside the iconic-megafauna allowlist (Hippopotamus, Blue Whale, etc. survive on archetype). Recommend retiring both and backfilling with strong inanimate subjects. Your call, since catalog entry retirement is on the CEO list.

### 4. "Today in Thiccctionary History" block on homepage
Below the daily entry, surface the entry from one year/quarter/month ago. Easy with existing entries.json, ~30 lines of JS. Adds a "discovery" moment for returning visitors and reduces the homepage's reliance on the recent-cards strip alone.

### 5. Newsletter signup needs prominence
Currently the only signup is the dark `.signup` band near the page bottom. By the time the reader scrolls there they've already decided to leave or stay. **Fix:** lightweight inline-signup on every entry page, just below the article share row. Two fields, one button. Already styled (`.inline-signup` exists in styles.css).

### 6. Personnel File <-> daily entry cross-link
When Bart's grievances reference entries (Liebherr T 282B, Saturn V, etc.), the entries should link back to the grievances and vice versa. Right now grievances live in isolation. Cross-linking deepens the world the reader is exploring. Editorial canon as a navigable graph, not a list.

### 7. Editorial Panels need an organic discovery surface
The 11 newspaper-style panels at /cartoons/ are some of the strongest brand content on the site. Nothing on the homepage points to them. **Fix:** below the Recent Entries strip on the homepage, add a "From the Art Desk" rail showing 2-3 rotating editorial panels.

## Strategic moves (your call)

Bigger directional choices. Each could change the trajectory of the site.

### A. Reader voting moment - Word of the Year / Quarter
The /rate page exists (811 lines, sophisticated head-to-head voting). It's invisible from the homepage. **Concept:** quarterly "Reader's Pick of the Quarter" landing page that surfaces the top voted entry, makes a moment of it (a printable certificate, a social card, an editorial board response from Bart). Turns a passive catalog into a participatory event. Drives social sharing.

### B. Annual "Thiccctionary Year in Review" PDF
End of year, generate a single bound PDF: 365 entries, the masthead, Bertram's signed letter, selected grievances, the year's editorial panels. Same PDF generator already builds the reference docs with Bertram's signature. Could be a free download AND a paid print-on-demand book. Test of the "print artifact" goal in book_roadmap.

### C. Submission showcase
The submit form exists. What happens to submissions? **Concept:** approved submissions surface on a public "Reader Submissions" page with the submitter's name + the catalog ruling. Bart's voice handles the rejections publicly. Sustained engagement loop, readers want to see if their thing made it.

### D. Branded merch from the editorial panels
The 11 panels are visually strong, on-brand, print-quality 1200x896 PNGs. T-shirts, posters, the Klencke Atlas on a notebook cover. Print-on-demand (Printful, Cotton Bureau) requires zero inventory. Bertram's signature on the back of a hoodie tag.

### E. Press kit + outreach
There's nothing at /press currently. Spin up a press kit (a brief, a few high-res cartoon panels, brand voice samples, the editorial board headshots). Distribute to one or two satire-friendly outlets (The Onion writers' room, Defector, Anne Helen Petersen). One pickup changes everything.

### F. Discord / community
Right now there's no place for fans of the brand to congregate. A Discord with channels per character (#bart-files, #spider-dispatches), the editorial board as roles, weekly office posts. Low cost, sustainable, deepens the brand.

### G. Search inside the catalogue
For 55 entries it's not critical. At 200+ it becomes essential. **Concept:** lightweight client-side search (Fuse.js on entries.json). Half a day of build.

## Brand observations

- **The world-building is the moat.** Filed Replies Vol. 2 worked because Bart, Constance, Spider, the green cabinet, the methodology memo all link to real documents now. This is uniquely Thiccctionary's. Lean into it.
- **The 1970s editorial-newspaper aesthetic is sharp and unusual.** Don't drift toward modern flat-design.
- **The "Hi Bart!" running joke is a brand asset.** Make Constance's corporate-speak a recurring punchline across columns and admin pages.
- **The cartoons (Editorial Panels) are doing more work than I think you realize.** They're the most shareable single-piece content on the site. Treat them as flagship product, not accessory.
- **The brand promise (inanimate things, never people) is being defended at code level now.** Auto-picker forbid list, editorial-sanity allowlist, critique gate test #8. That discipline is what keeps the satire crisp.
