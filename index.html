Thiccc Boeing

/thik BOH-ing/  n.
  1. 1. A widebody aircraft whose aft fuselage exhibits significantly more curvature than its fore fuselage; esp. one parked tail-toward the camera at golden hour.
  2. 2. colloq. Any commercial jet you'd describe as "built."
In a sentence —

"That 747 is straight-up a thiccc Boeing. The empennage on her? Architectural."

Etymology —

From thiccc (internet vernacular, c. 2015, "voluptuous; full-bodied," with an extra c for emphasis) + Boeing Company (Seattle-based aircraft manufacturer, est. 1916). First attested on Thiccctionary.com, May 2026.

A Note from the Editors

Thiccctionary is satire. Specifically, it is satire of how the internet talks about bodies, applied to literally anything but bodies — aircraft, fruit, freight vehicles, mid-century furniture, certain breeds of dog, and the occasional well-built bridge abutment.

No bodies are catalogued here. No people are subjects of entries. Every photograph is of an object. If we have done our job, you will laugh briefly and then describe a 1973 Cadillac DeVille with the word "stacked" for the rest of your natural life.

— The Editorial Board
