Avocado, Domestic

/a-vuh-KAH-doh/  n.
  1. 1. A pear-shaped fruit (Persea americana) of disproportionate hip-to-waist ratio, often photographed sliced for visual emphasis.
  2. 2. Any specimen exceeding 400g and exhibiting what botanists term 'a generous undercarriage'.
In a sentence —

"Cut it in half — this avocado is doing serious work."

Etymology —

From Spanish aguacate, from Nahuatl āhuacatl, originally meaning "testicle" — which, frankly, tracks.
