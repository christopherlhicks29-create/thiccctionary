Concrete Mixer

/KON-kreet MIK-ser/  n.
  1. 1. An industrial vehicle of contemplative rotundity, characterized by a single, slowly-rotating, drum-shaped midsection.
  2. 2. The platonic ideal of thicccness: all body, no apologies.
In a sentence —

"That mixer is a thiccc rig - eight cubic yards of slow-spinning confidence and a drum that hums when shes full."

Etymology —

From concrete (Latin concretus, "grown together") + mixer. The drum's rotation is, for our purposes, decorative.
