Eiffel Tower, Iron Lady

/EYE-fuhl TOW-er/  n.
  1. 1. A wrought iron structure of unparalleled posterior breadth, famed for its steadfast refusal to be anything but thiccc; often admired astern against a tumultuous sky.
  2. 2. colloq. A Parisian landmark that sets the standard for broad-based elegance, defying the Seine's attempts at subtlety.
In a sentence,

"He gazed at the Eiffel Tower, thiccc and unapologetic, as it held its ground against the gray clouds. Tourists recalibrated their cameras in awe."

Etymology,

From Gustave Eiffel (French engineer, b. 1832, d. 1923) + tower (Middle English < Old English torr, from Latin turris). A structure that needs no introduction, because it introduces itself.
