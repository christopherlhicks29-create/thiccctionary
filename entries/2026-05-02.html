Cement Truck

/sih-MENT truk/  n.
  1. 1. A heavily built vehicle designed for the transport of wet cement, boasting a robust exterior that exudes both strength and a certain je ne sais quoi.
  2. 2. Colloquially, a favorite amongst construction enthusiasts, often admired for its curvaceous body and the way it carries its load with remarkable poise.
In a sentence —

"When parked at the site, the cement truck's voluptuous silhouette drew the attention of all passerby, especially during twilight."

Etymology —

Derived from the Latin 'caementum', meaning 'chipped stone', combined with 'truck', a late Middle English term for a vehicle that brings home the goods—essentially, the original heavy-duty supplier of good vibes and gritty aesthetics.
