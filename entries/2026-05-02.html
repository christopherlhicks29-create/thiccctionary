Bulky Refrigerator

/BUL-ky re-FRIJ-er-ay-tor/  n.
  1. 1. An unapologetically large cooling appliance, often characterized by an ample physical presence and an inability to hide its considerable girth behind mere cabinetry.
  2. 2. A formidable kitchen companion that commands attention, particularly when situated beside a vintage bicycle, allowing observers to ponder the aesthetics of cold storage under low evening light.
In a sentence —

"Replaced our old fridge with this thiccc Frigidaire and the kitchen had to accommodate. Negative space? Gone."

Etymology —

Derived from the Latin refrigerare, meaning 'to cool,' coupled with the Middle English term bulk, which connotes largeness and a sense of undeniable presence. Together, they conjure an image of a colossal custodian of chilled goods.
