Ford F-450

/ford ef FOR-fif-tee/  n.
  1. 1. A pickup truck of such posterior magnitude that it requires dual rear wheels per side to remain in physical contact with the road.
  2. 2. slang. A vehicle that, were it sentient, would be described as 'thiccc with three Cs.'
In a sentence —

"Pulled into the diner lot in his F-450 — back end wider than the booth he sat in."

Etymology —

From Henry Ford (industrialist) + the model code for the heaviest-duty pickup in the lineup. The numerical suffix correlates positively with girth.
