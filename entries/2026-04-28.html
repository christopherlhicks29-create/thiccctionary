Plate IV. — A Brandywine of unusual proportions.

Heritage Tomato

/HAIR-ih-tij tuh-MAY-toh/  n.
  1. 1. A cultivar of Solanum lycopersicum whose girth defies its stem; structurally suspect, aesthetically blessed.
  2. 2. Any tomato that, if it were furniture, would require two people to move.
In a sentence —

"Three pounds. One tomato. They had to use two hands at the farmer's market."

Etymology —

From heritage (denoting ancestral cultivars) + tomato. The word "heritage" here implies that the tomato has a sense of history, gravitas, and lower-body presence.
