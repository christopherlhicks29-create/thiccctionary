Heritage Tomato

/HAIR-ih-tij tuh-MAY-toh/  n.
  1. 1. A variety of tomato plant cultivated from traditional, non-hybrid seeds, noted for its rich flavor and often irregular, voluptuous form.
  2. 2. colloq. A tomato with superior girth and juiciness, making lesser tomatoes feel self-conscious about their thin skins.
In a sentence —

"The Brandywine heritage tomato is undeniably thiccc, flaunting its curves like it's fresh off the vine runway."

Etymology —

The term 'heritage' in this context derives from the Old French 'eritage', meaning inheritance, reflecting the plant's lineage from older, preserved seed varieties. These tomatoes are the vintage fashion models of the produce aisle—pre-GMO and proud.
