# 851,000 of Anything Constitutes a Filing

**THE THICCC BEAT:** *the desk reacts. The Director of Editorial Operations reviews a world-record attempt the only way she knows how: as a count that has not yet been verified.*

By Constance Pribyl, Director of Editorial Operations

It has been brought to my attention that tomorrow evening, over Washington, D.C., a pyrotechnics firm intends to discharge approximately 851,000 fireworks in the space of forty minutes, and that the organizers have requested this be certified as a world record. The desk has asked me whether the display is thiccc. I have replied, in writing, that nothing is anything until somebody counts it.

The facts, as I have filed them. The current record stands at 810,904 fireworks, set ten years ago in the Philippines by a megachurch with a New Year's Eve and a budget, over the course of one hour, one minute, and thirty-two seconds. The Washington attempt proposes roughly 40,000 more fireworks in two-thirds of the time, launched from the National Mall, from West Potomac Park, and from eight barges anchored in the Potomac, which I note is the correct number of barges for a filing of this size. For scale: the Macy's display, traditionally the largest annual show in this country, runs sixty to eighty-five thousand shells. This proposes ten of those, stacked, in one evening.

Now, the complication, because this office does not skip complications. No individual firework in this display is thiccc. The largest shells are capped at ten inches by safety code, a formula this desk respects: one inch of shell equals one hundred feet of altitude, so a ten-inch shell requires a thousand feet of setback and a barrier. Ten inches does not meet catalogue minimums. If a single one of these shells arrived at intake, I would return it to sender with the standard letter.

But the desk has ruled on this structure before. Two weeks ago I filed [several hundred whale sharks off Isla Mujeres](/articles/thiccc-beat-2026-06-17-constance-whale-shark-afuera.html) as a single phenomenon with a quorum, on the grounds that mass which coordinates is not the same as mass which merely accumulates. The same doctrine applies here, with an amendment for tempo. The Philippine record worked out to roughly thirteen thousand fireworks a minute. This attempt proposes twenty-one thousand a minute. It is not merely more. It is more, faster, on one schedule, filed as one display. Abundance with a timetable is the pyrotechnic equivalent of girth with intention, and the catalogue was built to record exactly that.

However. The display has not happened. The count has not been verified. Guinness sends adjudicators to occasions like this, and so, in spirit, does this office. The desk does not certify numbers it has not seen, and I am not going to start on the largest number anyone has ever submitted to the sky.

**The ruling: Provisionally Thiccc.** The first provisional ruling in the history of this desk, effective upon a verified final count exceeding 810,904. If the tally comes in short, the ruling reverts, and the paperwork will say so, because the paperwork always says so. Filed under Pyrotechnics, Abundance Division, pending.

*Constance Pribyl, Director of Editorial Operations, who will be counting.*
