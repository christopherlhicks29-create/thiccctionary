The Editors

The Editorial Staff

Six contributors. One ongoing dispute over the Saturn V. The publication has been in continuous operation since 2026.

Editor-in-Chief

Margaret "Margie" Whitmore-Hessian

Inherited the publication from her late uncle Bertram, who founded it. Splits her time between the office, her vineyard in Sonoma, and Monaco. Has confused the Senior Cataloguer with the Junior Cataloguer in correspondence on at least two occasions. Believes Thiccctionary is on par with The Economist.

"The catalogue has been in continuous operation since my late uncle Bertram founded it. Possibly earlier. We're not sure."

Senior Cataloguer

Bartholomew "Bart" Pruss

Seventeen years on the job. Wrote the original style guide. Believes the catalogue should have stopped accepting submissions in 2014. Refuses to acknowledge the Saturn V as a vehicle. Has filed at least three formal objections to recent additions. Suspected of being the anonymous author of a 1990s newsletter called Girth Quarterly; he will not confirm or deny.

"The catalogue, properly understood, has never been about size. It is about structural commitment. I have explained this to the Junior Cataloguer on no fewer than eleven occasions."

Staff Writer

Eliza "Eli" Hartwell

The publication's most prolific contributor. Writes about everyday thiccc, urban infrastructure, foods of substance, and the bureaucratic decisions behind sidewalks. Has been known to insert footnotes that contradict the Senior Cataloguer's footnotes from the previous week. Refers to herself as "this writer" in print, which she finds amusing and the Junior Cataloguer finds professional.

"The fire hydrant outside the office is, in this writer's estimation, doing more for the catalogue than half the people working inside it."

Junior Cataloguer

Theodore "Teddy" Vance

Hired eighteen months ago. Still, in some sense, on probation in the Senior Cataloguer's eyes. Champions the Saturn V, modern industrial machinery, and what he calls "the next generation of catalogue subjects." Uses Latin phrases earnestly. Looks up to the Editor-in-Chief in a way the rest of the staff find quaint. The catalogue's most polite contributor.

"One submits, with respect, that the Saturn V is, in fact, a vehicle. Per definitionem."

Voice Editor / Reel Narrator

Hugh Drumm

Voices every Reel. Reads scripts literally, including stage directions when they appear. Speaks without contractions. Has no apparent inner life beyond the text in front of him. The other staff find him unsettling, but his Reel narrations are the publication's most popular content. Hired in 2014. The Senior Cataloguer insists Hugh was "already here." None of the other staff were present at the time.

"I have considered the script. The script states that the cement mixer is heavy. I will now record the line. The cement mixer is heavy."

Field Correspondent

"Spider" Hennessy

No first name on file. No photograph. Files pieces from the Hambach lignite mine at dawn, foundries in Sheffield, the deck of a supertanker outside Singapore. The phone number on record is in Macau. No one in the office has met Spider in person. The Senior Cataloguer insists Spider is a pseudonym for his brother-in-law; he has produced no evidence. The catalogue's most popular byline.

"Hambach. Pre-dawn. The wheel is turning. Slow. Slower than you think it is. I will explain when I get back."

A Note on Bylines

Field Reports carry the byline of the contributor. The publication's other content (daily entries, the catalogue itself) is the collective work of the staff. Disagreements between contributors are, in the Editor-in-Chief's view, "healthy and largely manageable."
