The Editorial Board

The people who maintain the catalogue, file the objections, and answer the letters. Most do not pick up the telephone.

Bertram Whitmore

Founder & Publisher

Founded the enterprise in 1974, ostensibly from a Manhattan office that rented for sixty-two dollars a month, fully serviced. Has not visited the offices since 1991. Currently aboard the Margaret IV, latitude undisclosed. Drafts memos on a typewriter, sends them by post. Believes the publication peaked in 1986.

Column · From the Boat (irregular)

Bartholomew Whitmore

Editor-in-Chief · Senior Cataloguer

Hired by Bertram in 1978 to catalog ships. Wrote the original style guide. Divorced in 2003 over a dispute about a credenza; the credenza is in his office now. Secretly proud of his 1991 Liebherr T 282B entry. Hates being called Bart. Will never admit he likes Teddy.

Read his Style Guide → · PDF ↓

Column · Filed Replies (Mailbag, weekly)

Constance Pribyl

Director of Editorial Operations

Hired by Bertram in 2003, by post, to modernize the publication's editorial operations. Has not modernized them. Maintains a cheerful demeanor about it. Refers to grievances as concerns, refusals as parking lot items, and disputes as opportunities to align. Has been promising a Q3 review of the coffee machine since 2018.

Maintains · The Personnel File

Eliza Hartwell

Staff Writer

Twenty-four. Came up from a town smaller than the readership. The only one in the office who actually finishes her work. Counts as a coping mechanism; the subject rotates. Lives in a fourth-floor walk-up.

Column · Field Notes (lists, weekly)

Theodore (“Teddy”)

Junior Cataloguer

Twenty-four. Recent graduate, philosophy minor. Genuinely believes the catalogue has importance and will defend it at dinner parties. Multiple ongoing obsessions: Roman aqueducts, Liberty Ships, the Saturn V, the Erie Canal locks. Writes entries twice as long as needed. Does not drink.

Column · Latinate Footnotes (occasional)

Margaret (“Margie”) Whitmore-Reuss

Society Correspondent

Bertram's brother's daughter. Does not maintain a permanent address; files from wherever she happens to be. Briefly married to a viscount, 2011 to 2012; would rather not discuss it. Strong opinions about cheese. Wears a scarf in every season including July.

Column · The Margie Column (when she returns calls)

Spider Hennessy

Foreign Correspondent · nom de plume

“Spider Hennessy” is the byline; the legal name is not on file. Files dispatches from rotating cities, datelined with the local hour (JAIPUR, 0530). No one at the office has met him. His expense reports arrive on time, correctly formatted, postmarked from a different city than the dispatch. The arrangement works.

Column · Spider Dispatches (one paragraph, irregular)

Hugh Drumm

Field Correspondent & Voiceover

Files telephone reports from industrial sites, refinery catwalks, and the occasional smokestack platform. Records audio when in town. Speaks slowly. Has been described as “documentary register, never winks.” Maintains a green field jacket and a working rotary handset.

Field reporting (irregular) · Voiceover (daily) · Cartoons (occasional)

Jon

Circulation Manager

Manages the circulation of the print edition. The publication has not printed since before his hire, a fact Jon regards as a job done and staying done; his weekly report reads “Circulation: 0. Steady.” and has never once been wrong. His position description lists eleven duties. Ten concern newsstand returns. The eleventh, unrepealed since 1978, concerns “the maintenance of delivery animals,” under which Jon keeps two racing burros, Registrar and Addendum, in competitive condition on company time. He places respectably at Fairplay. Requests outside the document are returned with a section citation. Requests inside it are executed flawlessly, to the office's quarterly astonishment. HR records list no surname; the form that would collect one is not among his duties to file.

Circulation report (weekly, one line) · Pack-burro standings (seasonal)

The Margaret IV remains at sea. The Senior Cataloguer remains at his desk.
