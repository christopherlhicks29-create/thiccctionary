Reference · The Foundational Entry

thiccc

/θɪk/  adj.
  1. 1. Possessing notable mass, density, or viscous substantiveness; esp. in a manner that warrants commentary.
  2. 2. Of an object, structure, or substance: of conspicuous girth or volume relative to the category to which it belongs.
  3. 3. colloq. So substantively constructed that the eye lingers; of a presence that fills its allotted space and then some.
In a sentence —

"The cement mixer rolled past the intersection with such thicccness that two pedestrians missed the walk signal entirely."

Etymology —

From thick (Old English þicce, "dense, stout, of considerable depth"), via thicc (early-2000s African-American Vernacular English and online discourse), with editorial doubling of the terminal c. The triple-c form, attested in scattered internet usage from the late 2010s, was formalized by the present publication in 2026 as the standard adjectival form for non-human applications.

foundational adjective editorial

An editorial note on the third c

The two-c spelling, thicc, is well documented. Merriam-Webster acknowledged it in 2018. Wiktionary has a clean entry. Know Your Meme has the timeline. Two c's are settled internet vernacular.

The third c is editorial. English does not formally double consonants for emphasis the way some languages do — but online writing improvised the convention, and we have escalated it. Two c's signal I am being a little online about this. Three c's signal I am fully committed to the bit and want you to know. It is the typographic equivalent of saying a thing twice while making prolonged eye contact.

The Thiccctionary uses three c's because the third c is what makes the project a project. A two-c "thicctionary" would be a one-off pun. The third c demands ongoing commitment. Every entry, every plate, every editorial decision honors that c.

The three senses we recognize

Girth. The original sense. A cement mixer is thiccc. A Chesterfield sofa is thiccc. A Boeing 747's rear fuselage is thiccc. The eye reads volume.

Density. Mass per unit space. A cinder block is thiccc. A bowling ball is thiccc. A wheel of aged Parmigiano-Reggiano is thiccc. The hand reads weight.

Viscosity. Substance suspended in liquid form. A milkshake from a competent diner is thiccc. Honey from a thoughtful apiary is thiccc. Engine oil after a long winter is thiccc. The spoon reads resistance.

An object qualifying in any one of these three senses warrants entry consideration. Objects qualifying in two or more proceed directly to the editorial review.

Frequently asked

What does thiccc mean?
Anything possessing notable mass, density, or viscous substantiveness, in a manner that warrants commentary. We apply it strictly to things, never to people.

Is it thicc or thiccc?
Both exist. Thicc is the settled internet form. Thiccc is the escalated form used here. The third c is the difference between participating in the slang and curating it.

Why three c's?
Because two c's are the language and three c's are the discipline. See above.

Can a person be thiccc?
Not in this dictionary. The original slang applies to body shape; our editorial rule restricts the word strictly to objects, structures, vehicles, and substances. People are off-limits. The joke depends on the discipline. See What Counts as Thiccc for the full editorial position.

How do you pronounce thiccc?
Identically to thick. /θɪk/. The extra c's are visual, not phonetic. They do their work on the page.
